# ComPASS Project Brief

## Goal

ComPASS 我的求职指南针 helps international students in Japan organize job hunting work that is usually scattered across Mynavi, Rikunabi, Indeed, LinkedIn, company career pages, school career centers, and personal notes. The product should make deadlines, company status, qualification study, interview preparation, and personal schedules easier to manage in one place.

## Target Users

- International students in Japan who are graduating from undergraduate programs, graduate school, language schools, vocational schools, or preparing for new graduate hiring.
- Students who already use major job platforms but need a personal command center for applications, deadlines, qualifications, and interview practice.
- Future secondary users: career support staff, senpai mentors, and community contributors who can share advice or events.

## Core Problem

Japanese job hunting has many moving parts: entry deadlines, company briefings, web tests, ES, interviews, qualifications, visa concerns, and different platform accounts. Students do not necessarily need another job board first. They need a reliable place to collect companies they care about, understand what to prepare next, and avoid missing dates.

## Core Loop

1. User adds or favorites companies from external sites or the built-in company database.
2. User records deadlines, briefings, interviews, status, notes, and links.
3. ComPASS turns those records into a calendar, deadline list, company board, and study priorities.
4. User practices tests, prepares ES/interviews, and updates application progress.

## Smallest Proof

A student can add five target companies, set two deadlines and one briefing, see them on the monthly calendar, practice a random test question, and export or back up their local data without losing track.

## Success Metrics

- A user can manage at least 10 companies and their deadlines without needing a spreadsheet.
- The personal calendar and deadline area show upcoming work clearly.
- The company search page helps discovery, but the personal page remains useful even when users find companies elsewhere.
- Data shown as real company/recruiting information has source and freshness labels.

## Non-Goals

- Do not become a full replacement for Mynavi, Rikunabi, Indeed, or LinkedIn in the MVP.
- Do not scrape or republish third-party review text.
- Do not claim foreign hiring ratio, recruiting status, or event dates are verified unless sourced.
- Do not build iOS before the web core loop is stable.
- Do not add account sync until the data model and privacy rules are defined.
